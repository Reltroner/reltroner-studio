# ⚓ Hargenbor — Guardian of the Central Seas of Asthortera

## Public Guardian, Abyssal Proxy, and Vertical Maritime Civilization

Hargenbor is one of the most distinctive sovereign nations on Asthortera: a maritime superpower surrounded by the Central Ocean, famous for its beaches, sailors, shipyards, seafood culture, underwater cities, sea legends, colossal navy, and the mysterious Hargenbor Bay.

Publicly, Hargenbor is known as:

> **The Guardian of the Central Seas of Asthortera.**

Strategically, however, Hargenbor holds a deeper and more dangerous role:

> **Hargenbor is the primary geopolitical proxy through which Nytherion Abyss projects influence against Reltronland, the state-level manifestation of Astralis Pinnacle.**

This does not make Hargenbor an illegal state, a collapsed puppet, or a civilization destined for destruction. Hargenbor remains a legitimate sovereign nation, internationally recognized, culturally rich, economically productive, and legally represented within the planetary diplomatic order of Asthortera.

Its contradiction is precisely what makes it powerful:

> **Hargenbor is beautiful enough to be admired, sovereign enough to be protected, competitive enough to matter, and abyssal enough to become useful to Nytherion.**

# 🗺️ Geographic Location

Hargenbor is situated east of Lenternow and stretches across the Central Ocean through a massive loop-coast formation.

Unlike ordinary coastal nations, Hargenbor does not share land borders with other states. Its entire national geography is surrounded by oceanic frontiers.

## The Hargenbor Coastal Loop

Hargenbor’s defining geographic structure is known as:

> **The Hargenbor Coastal Loop**

This loop forms a continuous maritime boundary around the nation, giving Hargenbor an almost complete civilizational dependence on the sea.

The country includes:

* long coastlines
* white and blue-sand beaches
* tropical resort belts
* storm-facing cliffs
* hidden bays
* lighthouse chains
* deep sea caves
* shipbuilding harbors
* underwater rail corridors
* coral shelf settlements
* abyssal military zones
* trench-floor sovereignty zones

Because Hargenbor has no land borders, its political psychology differs from land-based nations. Its citizens do not primarily think in terms of walls, roads, borders, plains, or mountains. They think in terms of tides, ports, storms, currents, routes, depth, navigation, and survival.

# 🌊 Vertical Oceanic Sovereignty

Hargenbor’s sovereignty is not limited to land and surface waters.

It possesses one of the most advanced forms of maritime jurisdiction on Asthortera:

> **Vertical Oceanic Sovereignty**

This means Hargenbor legally, militarily, economically, and infrastructurally governs the oceanic column from sea level to the deepest sovereign trench floor.

Its claimed oceanic depth extends:

> **From 0 meters at sea level to approximately 36 kilometers below sea level.**

This vertical sovereignty gives Hargenbor control over:

* surface ports
* coastal waters
* coral shelf cities
* submarine routes
* underwater transportation
* abyssal research bases
* deep-sea military corridors
* trench-floor installations
* Nytherion anomaly monitoring zones
* classified Central Gate containment sectors

This is the core reason Hargenbor is more than a maritime nation.

It is a civilization built downward.

# ⚠️ Naval Strategies of Hargenbor

## Fogwave Tactic

A tactic that uses natural mist, abyssal distortion, and maritime illusions for ambush warfare.

## Spiral Ocean Formation

A defensive spiral ring deployed around Hargenbor Bay and key naval corridors.

## Controlled Anti-Rogue Abyssal Arsenal

Hargenbor’s carriers and destroyers are equipped with:

* exorcist bullets
* photon-lumen weaponry
* abyssal suppression systems
* anti-illusion sonar
* dimensional shielding
* fog-piercing sensors

This arsenal is officially described as anti-Nytherion defense.

In practice, it is more precise to say that Hargenbor uses it to contain rogue abyssal entities, stabilize abyssal zones, and control the balance between sovereignty and Nytherion leverage.

This avoids contradiction:

> **Hargenbor does not seek to destroy Nytherion. Hargenbor seeks to manage, exploit, contain, and survive its relationship with Nytherion.**

# 🧩 Internal Political Factions

Hargenbor is not politically monolithic.

Its internal factions create much of its narrative complexity.

## 1. Sovereign Maritime Government

The official state apparatus.

It seeks to maintain:

* sovereignty
* international legitimacy
* maritime prosperity
* naval strength
* economic competitiveness
* diplomatic recognition

Not all members of this government are malicious. Many sincerely believe they are protecting Hargenbor’s national interests.

## 2. Abyssal Strategic Bloc

The faction most aligned with Nytherion.

It believes Hargenbor’s destiny is to become the maritime rival of Reltronland.

Its members see the Abyss not as corruption, but as a source of depth, power, and competitive advantage.

## 3. Tourism and Coastal Class

This class includes:

* resort owners
* fishermen
* chefs
* musicians
* diving guides
* port workers
* coastal artists
* cruise operators
* seafood merchants

Most of them experience Hargenbor as a beautiful maritime nation and may not understand the deeper abyssal politics.

## 4. Deep-Sea Technocrats

Engineers, submarine architects, oceanic scientists, underwater planners, and pressure-system designers.

They are pragmatic.

Some cooperate with abyssal technology because it works.

## 5. Old Sailor Traditionalists

They respect the sea but distrust Nytherion.

They believe the ocean should be honored, not exploited by abyssal ideology.

Many are connected to Thalassara, Spiritbound Battleships, and ancestral sailor oaths.

## 6. Hidden Anti-Abyss Resistance

A quiet minority inside Hargenbor.

They believe Hargenbor has been spiritually hijacked by Nytherion’s influence.

They do not necessarily support Reltronland politically, but they reject the idea that Hargenbor should become the perfected proxy of the Abyss.

# 🌐 Relationship with Other Nations

## Reltronland

Reltronland views Hargenbor as a Nytherion proxy and a long-term ideological threat.

However, Reltronland cannot simply destroy, annex, or delegitimize Hargenbor because Hargenbor is a recognized sovereign state.

This creates a strategic dilemma:

> **How can Astralis confront Nytherion’s proxy without violating the sovereignty of a legitimate nation?**

Reltronland must rely on:

* diplomacy
* intelligence
* anti-illusion doctrine
* economic resilience
* cultural clarity
* internal anti-stagnation systems
* strategic patience

## Depcutland

Depcutland documents Hargenbor’s abyssal history through archives, testimonies, maritime records, and memory preservation.

It often warns that Hargenbor’s official narrative hides deeper memory distortions.

## Lenternow

Lenternow cooperates with Hargenbor through lighthouse networks, navigation systems, coastal illumination, and maritime guidance.

However, Lenternow fears abyssal fog interference and Nytherion distortion in Hargenborian waters.

## Pasgerflit

Pasgerflit maintains air-sea tourism routes with Hargenbor.

It benefits from Hargenbor’s tourism economy but monitors security risks near fog zones, Baythorne, and abyssal corridors.

## Taramistry

Taramistry studies Hargenborian marine biochemistry, abyssal organisms, and pressure-adapted life forms.

Some Taramistry scientists worry that research cooperation with Hargenbor risks ethical contamination from Nytherion-linked biology.

## Astrostelia

Astrostelia cooperates with Hargenbor on ocean-cosmic resonance, deep-sea observatories, and cosmic-oceanic signal research.

## Kalgered

Kalgered acts as a diplomatic moderator.

It insists that Hargenbor’s sovereignty must be respected even when spiritual or abyssal influence is suspected.

# ✅ Final Canon Identity

Hargenbor is a sovereign loop-coast maritime civilization of Asthortera, renowned for its sailors, beaches, underwater cities, shipbuilding, naval strength, and vertical oceanic sovereignty from sea level to the 36-kilometer trench floor.

It is publicly known as the Guardian of the Central Seas and internationally recognized as a legitimate member of the United Assembly of Asthortera.

However, beneath its legal sovereignty and admired beauty, Hargenbor functions as the primary geopolitical proxy of Nytherion Abyss. Nytherion does not seek to destroy Hargenbor. It cultivates Hargenbor as a beautiful, competitive, maritime counterweight against Reltronland, the direct state-level manifestation of Astralis Pinnacle.

Through Hargenbor, Nytherion gains leverage over:

* Central Ocean trade
* maritime logistics
* underwater infrastructure
* tourism soft power
* abyssal military depth
* Hargenbor Bay
* the Central Gate of Nytherion Abyss
* the psychological appeal of comfort disguised as paradise

Hargenbor’s tragedy is not that it is weak.

Its tragedy is that it is useful.

# Final Assessment

Hargenbor is one of the most complex nations in Asthortera.

It is not a simple villain state.

It is not merely a victim.

It is not only a guardian.

It is a beautiful, sovereign, internationally legitimate maritime civilization whose geography, economy, navy, underwater cities, and cultural softness make it the perfect leverage point for Nytherion Abyss.

This makes Hargenbor essential to the larger conflict between Astralis and Nytherion.

Reltronland embodies the hard discipline of ascent.

Hargenbor embodies the seductive gravity of descent.

Between them lies one of the central tensions of Asthortera:

> **Can civilization remain free when comfort itself becomes geopolitical power?**
